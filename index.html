He tracked her Diet Coke habits, pasta preferences, and political views for 8 months. The conclusion? "She is poetry."

By Niv | February 18, 2026 | 9:28am

Forget flowers. Forget chocolates. Fellas, the bar has just been raised to the stratosphere, and frankly, you’re doomed.

Meet Niv. He’s in a long-distance relationship. He’s got a laptop. And apparently, he has absolutely zero chill.

After eight months of dating his girlfriend, Aaradhya, from 2,083 kilometers away, Niv didn't just tell her he loved her. He wrote a 47-page academic research paper titled “A Comprehensive Analysis of Aaradhya: An 8-Month Longitudinal Study on Human Excellence.”

Yes, really.

“I realized I wasn’t just falling in love,” Niv told The Post. “I was conducting a longitudinal observational study. And the subject was producing data that defied standard deviation.”

The "Diet Coke" Incident

Every scientific breakthrough has a catalyst. For Newton, it was an apple. For Niv? It was a Diet Coke.

According to Section 4.5.1 of his massive treatise, Aaradhya—who previously didn't care for the stuff—tried a Diet Coke simply because Niv mentioned he liked it. Now? She loves it.

“Small gesture. Large implication,” the paper notes. Niv calls it statistically significant proof of her kindness. We call it simp behavior of the highest order—and honestly? It’s adorable.

The Data Don't Lie

Niv, who admits in his own methodology section that he has “completely lost objectivity” because he finds the subject “hot,” broke down Aaradhya’s perfection into hard data:

  • Marvel Knowledge: Comprehensive.
  • Politics: Leftist (which the paper notes is “correct and hot”).
  • Pasta Wars: She likes penne/pesto. He likes spaghetti. The study concludes this “minor incompatibility” is survivable.

But the real kicker comes in the section on Art. Aaradhya writes poetry. But Niv wasn't satisfied with just reviewing her work.

“She writes poetry,” the paper argues in a move that would make Shakespeare blush, “but the meta-analysis confirms she is poetry.”

Compromised Researcher

Niv’s paper is filled with charts, graphs, and citations, but he’s the first to admit his funding comes from “excessive emotional investment.”

In the "Limitations" section, he writes: “Researcher finds subject hot and cannot maintain objectivity. This finding affects all subsequent analysis.”

So, has the subject read the findings yet?

“Not yet,” Niv says. “That’s what this article is for. To tell her it exists.”

Aaradhya, if you’re reading this: He analyzed 60% of his phone’s storage worth of voice notes for you. Marry him.

ABSTRACT

Human excellence research has traditionally focused on achievement metrics. This study examines a unique subject, Aaradhya, whose excellence transcends conventional measurement. Through an 8-month longitudinal observational study conducted across a distance of 2,083km, we systematically documented multi-faceted excellence across domains: intellectual capacity, creative output (poetry), political alignment, aesthetic beauty, and dietary compatibility. Data sources included >10,000 text messages, voice notes consuming 60% of researcher device storage, and n=1 critical video observation. Results indicate the subject demonstrates exceptional performance across all domains. Specifically, the "Diet Coke Incident" reveals high character strength; political alignment (Leftist) correlates significantly with attractiveness (p<0.001); and the subject was found to simultaneously write poetry and be poetry. The study concludes that the subject is perfect. The researcher acknowledges that objectivity was compromised by the finding that the subject is hot, yet maintains the validity of the conclusions.
1. Introduction
The quantification of love has long eluded the scientific community. While poets have attempted to describe it and biologists to dissect it, few have attempted to document it with the rigor of a 47-page longitudinal study. This paper seeks to fill that gap by analyzing the subject, Aaradhya, observed over a period of eight months from a distance of 2,083 kilometers.
The primary research question driving this study was simple yet profound: Is she as perfect as she seems? Secondary questions investigated the correlation between political beliefs and attractiveness, and whether pasta preference incompatibilities (penne vs. spaghetti) constitute a threat to long-term viability.
Historical context suggests that long-distance relationships (LDRs) often suffer from a lack of observational data. However, this study posits that distance acts as a clarifying lens. Without physical proximity to cloud judgment with pheromones alone, the researcher was forced to evaluate the subject based on character, voice, intellect, and the contents of 180GB of digital correspondence.
2. Methodology
2.1 Data Collection
Data was collected from June 2025 to February 2026. Sources included:
  • Textual Analysis: Over 10,000 messages analyzed for wit, kindness, and Marvel Universe references.
  • Audio Analysis: Voice notes currently occupying 60% of the researcher's phone storage.
  • Behavioral Observation: The subject was observed in various states (stressed, happy, studying biology).
2.2 The Distance Variable
The constant distance of 2,083km acted as a unique filter. Without the distraction of physical proximity, the researcher was forced to focus on the substance of the subject's character, intellect, and voice. The researcher admits that the sample size (n=1) is small, but argues that the subject is unique, rendering larger sample sizes irrelevant.
2.3 Limitations of the Researcher
It must be noted at the outset: The researcher finds the subject hot. This finding, confirmed early in the data collection phase via FaceTime (n=1), has compromised researcher objectivity. As stated in the Funding Statement, this research is self-funded through excessive emotional investment.

A potential area of friction was identified regarding pasta preferences. The subject prefers Penne (a tubular shape) with Pesto. The researcher prefers Spaghetti (a strand shape).
Mathematical modeling suggests that while the shapes are geometrically incompatible, the emotional compatibility remains high. The researcher has committed to consuming Pesto, despite previous reservations, citing the "Reciprocity Principle" derived from the Diet Coke Incident.
3.6 Marvel Universe Proficiency
Standardized testing (pop quizzes over FaceTime) revealed a comprehensive knowledge of the Marvel Cinematic Universe (MCU). This shared cultural lexicon provides a high-bandwidth channel for communication and memes.
4. Discussion
4.1 On Perfection
The term "perfect" is rarely used in scientific literature due to the impossibility of absolute flawlessness. However, in this study, "perfect" is defined as "exactly as she should be." By this definition, the subject achieves the metric with p<0.000001 significance.
The researcher asked the control question: "Would you change anything?" The answer, consistent across all 8 months, was "No." This includes the Orange Allergy, which creates a specific constraint on fruit purchasing but adds to the unique character profile of the subject.
4.2 Bias and Validity
Critics may argue that the researcher is "completely compromised" or "obsessed." The researcher accepts these labels. The finding that the subject is hot affects all subsequent analysis, yet the data (Marvel knowledge, kindness, biology expertise) remains objective.
5. Conclusion
After 47 pages of analysis, the conclusion is irrefutable. Aaradhya is beautiful, intelligent, politically correct, and the best phenomenon observed in the researcher’s lifetime.
The researcher recommends immediate and permanent retention of the subject. Future research will focus on closing the 2,083km gap to 0km to allow for in-person replication of these findings.
