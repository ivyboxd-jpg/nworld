LOVE SICK! Guy writes massive dissertation just to prove girlfriend is ‘perfect’

He tracked her Diet Coke habits, pasta preferences, and political views for 8 months. The conclusion? "She is poetry."

By Niv | February 18, 2026 | 9:28am

Forget flowers. Forget chocolates. Fellas, the bar has just been raised to the stratosphere, and frankly, you’re doomed.

Meet Niv. He’s in a long-distance relationship. He’s got a laptop. And apparently, he has absolutely zero chill.

After eight months of dating his girlfriend, Aaradhya, from 2,083 kilometers away, Niv didn't just tell her he loved her. He wrote a comprehensive academic research paper titled “A Analysis of Aaradhya: An 8-Month Longitudinal Study on Human Excellence.”

Yes, really.

“I realized I wasn’t just falling in love,” Niv told The Post. “I was conducting a longitudinal observational study. And the subject was producing data that defied standard deviation.”

The "Diet Coke" Incident

Every scientific breakthrough has a catalyst. For Newton, it was an apple. For Niv? It was a Diet Coke. According to Section 4.5.1 of his massive treatise, Aaradhya—who previously didn't care for the stuff—tried a Diet Coke simply because Niv mentioned he liked it. Now? She loves it.

“Small gesture. Large implication,” the paper notes. Niv calls it statistically significant proof of her kindness.

The Data Don't Lie

Niv, who admits in his own methodology section that he has “completely lost objectivity” because he finds the subject “hot,” broke down Aaradhya’s perfection into hard data:

  • Marvel Knowledge: Comprehensive.
  • Politics: Leftist (which the paper notes is “correct and hot”).
  • Pasta Wars: She likes penne/pesto. He likes spaghetti. The study concludes this “minor incompatibility” is survivable.

But the real kicker comes in the section on Art. Aaradhya writes poetry. But Niv wasn't satisfied with just reviewing her work. “She writes poetry,” the paper argues, “but the meta-analysis confirms she is poetry.”

ABSTRACT

Traditional metrics of human excellence often rely on standardized testing or career achievement. This study proposes an alternative framework: The Aaradhya Metric. Through an 8-month longitudinal observational study conducted across a distance of 2,083km, we systematically documented multi-faceted excellence across domains: intellectual capacity, creative output (poetry), political alignment, aesthetic beauty, and dietary compatibility. Data sources included >10,000 text messages, voice notes consuming 60% of researcher device storage, and n=1 critical video observation. Results indicate the subject demonstrates exceptional performance across all domains. Specifically, the "Diet Coke Incident" reveals high character strength; political alignment (Leftist) correlates significantly with attractiveness (p<0.001); and the subject was found to simultaneously write poetry and be poetry. The study concludes that the subject is perfect. The researcher acknowledges that objectivity was compromised by the finding that the subject is hot, yet maintains the validity of the conclusions.
1. Introduction
The quantification of love has long eluded the scientific community. While poets have attempted to describe it and biologists to dissect it, few have attempted to document it with the rigor of a longitudinal study. This paper seeks to fill that gap by analyzing the subject, Aaradhya, observed over a period of eight months from a distance of 2,083 kilometers.
The primary research question driving this study was simple yet profound: Is she as perfect as she seems? Secondary questions investigated the correlation between political beliefs and attractiveness, and whether pasta preference incompatibilities (penne vs. spaghetti) constitute a threat to long-term viability.
Historical context suggests that long-distance relationships (LDRs) often suffer from a lack of observational data. However, this study posits that distance acts as a clarifying lens. Without physical proximity to cloud judgment with pheromones alone, the researcher was forced to evaluate the subject based on character, voice, intellect, and the contents of 180GB of digital correspondence.
1.1 Significance of the Study
This research is significant because it challenges the prevailing notion that "nobody is perfect." Through rigorous data collection, we aim to prove that this specific subject is a statistical outlier. Furthermore, the study provides a roadmap for future cohabitation protocols, specifically regarding the handling of citrus fruits (see Appendix B: Orange Allergy).
2. Literature Review
2.1 Theories of Love and Distance
Sternberg (1986) proposed the Triangular Theory of Love, consisting of intimacy, passion, and commitment. This study applies Sternberg's model to the subject, finding maximum values in all three vertices. However, Sternberg failed to account for the "Diet Coke Factor," a novel variable introduced in this research.
2.2 The Aesthetics of Distance
Trope and Liberman (2010) argue that psychological distance promotes abstract thinking. In the case of Aaradhya, the physical distance allowed the researcher to form an abstract representation of her "Perfection," which was subsequently confirmed by concrete data points (video calls and voice notes).
2.3 Pasta Compatibility Frameworks
Previous studies (Boyardee, 1999) suggest that pasta shape preference is a trivial variable in relationship success. However, more recent research by Niv (2025) indicates that the Penne vs. Spaghetti dichotomy represents a fundamental worldview difference—one centered on structure vs. chaos. This study explores how such differences can coexist.
3. Methodology
3.1 Data Collection Protocols
Data was collected continuously from June 1, 2025, to February 18, 2026. The dataset is comprised of diverse media formats, ensuring a holistic view of the subject. Sources included:
  • Textual Analysis: Over 10,000 text messages were archived and analyzed for wit, kindness, and timing.
  • Audio Analysis: Voice notes currently occupying 60% of the researcher's phone storage. These notes were analyzed for tone, laughter frequency, and narrative structure.
  • Visual Observation: FaceTime calls (n > 200) provided data on aesthetic consistency.
3.2 The "Hotness" Bias (Disclosure)
It must be noted at the outset: The researcher finds the subject hot. This finding, confirmed early in the data collection phase via FaceTime (n=1), has compromised researcher objectivity. As stated in the Funding Statement, this research is self-funded through excessive emotional investment. All findings must be viewed through the lens of this acknowledged bias, though the researcher maintains that the bias is a result of the data, not the cause of it.

4. Results
4.1 The Diet Coke Incident
In the early stages of the study (Month 1), the researcher mentioned a preference for Diet Coke. The subject, who had no prior affinity for the beverage, voluntarily consumed one to understand the researcher's preference.
Finding: She now loves it.
Significance: This event serves as a primary behavioral marker. It demonstrates openness to experience and a willingness to engage with the researcher's preferences. It was a small gesture with large implications for compatibility. It is statistically significant (p < 0.001) evidence of kindness.

4.2 Political Alignment Assessment
Analysis of conversations regarding current events reveals the subject holds Leftist political beliefs. The researcher cross-referenced this with his own preferences to determine compatibility. The data indicates a 100% match. The researcher notes in his field journal: "Correct political beliefs. Researcher finds this attractive." This alignment suggests a shared moral framework, which is essential for long-term viability (Gottman, 1999).

4.3 The Poetry Paradox
The subject is a known writer of poetry. However, extensive qualitative analysis suggests a dual phenomenon. The subject does not merely produce poetry; the subject is poetry.
This conclusion is supported by the rhythmic nature of her voice notes and the metaphorical richness of her existence. While academically unconventional, the researcher insists this finding is accurate. The distinction between the artist and the art has collapsed in this specific instance. To read her work is to know her mind; to know her mind is to read a poem.
4.4 Dietary Compatibility Matrix
A potential area of friction was identified regarding pasta preferences. The subject prefers Penne (a tubular shape) with Pesto. The researcher prefers Spaghetti (a strand shape).
Mathematical modeling suggests that while the shapes are geometrically incompatible, the emotional compatibility remains high. The researcher has committed to consuming Pesto, despite previous reservations, citing the "Reciprocity Principle" derived from the Diet Coke Incident.
4.5 Medical Considerations: The Orange Allergy
The subject possesses a documented allergy to oranges. Observations show she manages this limitation without complaint, further evidencing resilience.
Future meal planning protocols have been adjusted to "Zero Oranges." The researcher notes that this is a "small price to pay for perfection."
4.6 Marvel Universe Proficiency
Standardized testing (pop quizzes over FaceTime) revealed a comprehensive knowledge of the Marvel Cinematic Universe (MCU). This shared cultural lexicon provides a high-bandwidth channel for communication and memes. It suggests that weekends can be safely allocated to movie marathons without need for exposition.
5. Discussion
5.1 Defining Perfection
The term "perfect" is rarely used in scientific literature due to the impossibility of absolute flawlessness. However, in this study, "perfect" is defined as "exactly as she should be." By this definition, the subject achieves the metric with p<0.000001 significance.
5.2 The "Would Change Anything" Test
The researcher conducted a thought experiment: "Would you change anything about the subject?" The answer, consistent across all 8 months, was "No." This includes the Orange Allergy, which creates a specific constraint on fruit purchasing but adds to the unique character profile of the subject.
5.3 Addressing Bias
Critics may argue that the researcher is "completely compromised" or "obsessed." The researcher accepts these labels. The finding that the subject is hot affects all subsequent analysis, yet the data (Marvel knowledge, kindness, biology expertise) remains objective. The researcher argues that love is not a blinding force, but a magnifying one—allowing him to see details (like the way she drinks Diet Coke) that others miss.
6. Conclusion
After extensive analysis, the conclusion is irrefutable. Aaradhya is beautiful, intelligent, politically correct, and the best phenomenon observed in the researcher’s lifetime.
The researcher recommends immediate and permanent retention of the subject. Future research will focus on closing the 2,083km gap to 0km to allow for in-person replication of these findings. Until then, data collection will continue via digital means.
